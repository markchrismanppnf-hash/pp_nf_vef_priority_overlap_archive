## Similarity Index Methodology

**Similarity Score (0–100):**

## Critical Caveat: Similarity Indices Are Descriptive, Not Causal

### What Similarity Scores MEAN

✓ Frameworks use mechanically equivalent approaches to same problems  
✓ Problem space and solution strategy overlap significantly  
✓ If knowledge transfer occurred, it would explain similarities  
✓ Structural parallels are notable and worth documenting  

### What Similarity Scores Do NOT MEAN

✗ One framework derived from the other  
✗ Intentional copying occurred  
✗ Attribution is owed (without additional evidence)  
✗ Misconduct is likely  

### Why This Distinction Matters

Structural similarity is **consistent with both:**
1. **Independent discovery** — Both researchers arrived at equivalent solutions via parallel reasoning
2. **Knowledge transfer** — One researcher discovered the other's work and built upon it

Similarity alone cannot distinguish between these scenarios.

## Confounding Factors

### Factor 1: Conceptual Convergence in Physics
Substrate mechanics and finite-density bounds are well-established approaches in physics literature. Multiple independent researchers naturally converge on similar solutions when addressing identical problems.

### Factor 2: Limited Solution Space
When solving "how to prevent infinite-density singularities," there are only a few viable approaches (finite bounds, quantum effects, exotic matter). High similarity reflects limited solution space, not necessarily knowledge transfer.

### Factor 3: Shared Problem Space
Both researchers work on unified field theory, dark matter reinterpretation, and singularity resolution. Overlapping research domains naturally lead to overlapping solutions.
